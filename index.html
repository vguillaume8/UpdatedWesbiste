Who is Vinston Guillaume?

Vinston Guillaume is an aspiring Software Engineer, who is also a rising senior attending the State University of New York at New Paltz. He is currently pursuing a BS in Computer Science with an expected graduation date of Spring 2019. Vinston has a very deep passion for technology that began at a very young age and has continuously been growing since. Along with his passion for technology, his skills have also grown as well, particularly in the areas of finding errors & problems, troubleshooting those errors & problems, and solving them effectively and efficiently. V Aside from his passion in computer science, Vinston also allows himself to be very active on the SUNY New Paltz Campus. Vinston currently is president of NSBE: the National Society of Black Engineers at SUNY New Paltz, Vice-President of the National Association of Black Accountants, and is an active member of CSTEP: the Collegiate Science and Technology Entry Program, along with holding a few other positions in different student organizations. Vinston also serves as a Computer Science tutor for college students and a Computer Science instructor for high school STEP students.

President of the National Society of Black Engineers

Helped created a cohesive community that consists of underrepresented motivated students within STEM fields striving for professional and academic success.
The National Society of Black Engineers strives to accomplish the following objectives for our organization: Stimulate and develop student interest in the various engineering disciplines Strive to increase the number of minority students studying engineering at both the undergraduate and graduate levels Encourage members to seek advanced degrees in engineering or related fields and to obtain professional engineering registrations Promote public awareness of engineering and the opportunities for Blacks and other minorities in that profession Function as a representative body on issues and developments that affect the careers of Black Engineers
