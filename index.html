Who is Vinston Guillaume?

Vinston Guillaume is an Software Engineer. He is a recent graduate and alumni of the State University of New York at New Paltz. He graduated with a Computer Science Degree, May 19, 2019. Vinston has a very deep passion for technology that began at a very young age and has continuously been growing since. Along with his passion for technology, his skills have also grown as well, particularly in the areas of finding errors & problems, troubleshooting those errors & problems, and solving them effectively and efficiently. Vinston is now focusing on increasing his knowledge with the Swift programming language and developing IOS applications.

SUNY New Paltz Alumni

Vinston Guillaume graduated May 19, 2019 from the State University of New York at New Paltz. At SUNY New Paltz, Vinston was president of NSBE: the National Society of Black Engineers at SUNY New Paltz, Vice-President of the National Association of Black Accountants, and is an active member of CSTEP: the Collegiate Science and Technology Entry Program, along with holding a few other positions in different student organizations. Vinston also serves as a Computer Science tutor for college students and a Computer Science instructor for high school STEP students. Vinston also won 1st Place at the annual Computer Science Senior Projects Expo for his work on developing the EOP P.I.L.L.A.R. Tracking System.

Former President of the National Society of Black Engineers

As a former president, Vinston helped 20+ SUNY New Paltz Students acquire full-time jobs and internships. Helped created a cohesive community that consists of underrepresented, motivated students within STEM fields striving for professional and academic success.
The National Society of Black Engineers strives to accomplish the following objectives for our organization: Stimulate and develop student interest in the various engineering disciplines. Strive to increase the number of minority students studying engineering at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. Encourage members to seek advanced degrees in engineering or related fields and to obtain professional engineering registrations. Promote public awareness of engineering and the opportunities for Blacks and other minorities in that profession. Function as a representative body on issues and developments that affect the careers of Black Engineers.
